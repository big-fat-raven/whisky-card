Mouthfeel (Check all that apply)
Tactile sensations on the palate — not flavors, but how the liquid physically feels. These often trace back to specific production choices: oily or waxy textures from worm tub condensers, grip and tannin from sherry casks, a fizzing or prickling sensation from high-strength new make.
Dominant Chemical Families (Rank top 3 by relative prominence)
Rather than naming individual flavors, rank the three compound families that most define this whisky's character — esters (fruity, floral), aldehydes (cereal, grassy), phenols (smoky, medicinal), and so on. Think in terms of which molecular group is driving the experience, not just what fruit or flower it smells like.
Probable Process Drivers (Sensory first, technical second)
These scales reflect the production choices most likely responsible for what you're tasting.
